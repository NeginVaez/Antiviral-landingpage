Antivirals are mostly virus-specific

Existing antivirals attempt to interfere with the viral
replication process inside host cells.

This makes them more prone to lose effectiveness as viruses mutate.
These approaches also have a greater risk
of toxic side-effects

Technology

Our antivirals employ a
unique machanism of action

Contray to existing antivirals, this new approach
attacks virus particles as they circulate outside of host body.

Effective anytime circulating virions are present

As a result, our antivirals are effective regardless of the state of the infection,
and have been shown to provide significantly longer therapeutic windows
than existing drugs.

Works equally well for RNA viruses and DNA viruses,
as it depends on the binding site sought by the virus
